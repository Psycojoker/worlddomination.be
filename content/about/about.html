Neutrinet is member of [Gitoyen](http://www.gitoyen.net/). ## [Political Memory](http://memopol.org) I'm the main developer of the Political Memory. It is a tool whose purpose is to allow a NGO (or anyone in fact) to publish the voting recommendation they have sent to elected representatives, the tool then gather the list of elected representatives, their votes and generate a listing of how well those elected representatives have been following those voting recommendations. Example: [la Quadrature du Net](https://laquadrature.net) told the Members of the European Parliament that they should vote for [ACTA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anti-Counterfeiting_Trade_Agreement) because it is destroying our freedom of the Internet (and for many other reasons) and [here you can get the result](https://memopol.lqdn.fr/europe/parliament/vote/acta_final/) (no, I'm not happy by the current display). The whole point of this tool is that if you think that, for example, la Quadrature du Net represent you interests of Internet related subjects, you can go of the Political Memory of la Quadrature du Net and see how well your elected representatives are effectively representing you. This is a very valuable information that you don't have for the moment and that allow you to see your representatives through concrete informations that are important for you and that YOU have chosen, not through their marketing campaign or what the press is saying about them. Of course, the objective is a wider adoption of this tool by politically involved NGO or individuals. This is my current focus since, as today, la Quadrature du Net is the only one to use it. It's a tool, initially made by [Gibus](http://pascontent.sedrati-dinet.net/) for [la Quadrature du Net](https://laquadrature.net), of which I've been the main dev of the second (and current) version with a lot of [awesome contributors](https://github.com/Psycojoker/memopol2/blob/master/CREDITS.txt). I'm currently focusing my effort of making this tool generic and usable by humans with the idea of seen a wider adoption of it. ### [Toutatis](http://toutatis.mm.staz.be/) and [Compotista](http://compotista.mm.staz.be/) As part of the Political Memory new generic version, I've written 2 tools with [staz](http://staz.be) to ease this work and extract things from the Political Memory that shouldn't be in it. Toutatis is a tool which roles is to make the matching between voting results produced by the European Parliament which only contains the last name of the Member of the European Parliament and a real member of the European Parliament. Compotista is a tool to convert the Member of the European Parliament data coming from [parltrack](http://parltrack.euwiki.org/) into the *representative format* that we have written with [stf](http://www.ctrlc.hu/~stef/blog/). ## [NURPA](http://nurpa.be) NURPA is a citizen group that defend our Fundamental Freedom of the Internet.
